Current Studies: Information Systems & Computer System Fundamentals

Information Systems

The information systems unit is graded via a group project which takes place over the first year. This means that the skills learnt through the course can be applied to a 'real world' scenario to give it context. The content of this unit revolves around the use of computer/information systems in organisations (mainly business's) and the skills needed to build and maintain these systems. As well as this the group project means that throughout the year, communication and teamworking skills are developed these are useful throughout university and employment. Some of the topics studies through the year are:

  • Database design/development
  • Systems analysis and design techniques
  • E-commerce theory
  • Use of information systems
The image shown on the left of this section is of a use-case diagram, this is a technique used in 'Systems design' to define the interactions between the users and the (at this point theoretical) information system. Use-case diagrams are an example of the kind of content this topic includes.

Computer System Fundamentals

The unit 'Computer System fundamentals' provides an introduction to the fundamental principles and mathematics that underpin the design of Computer Systems. The subject is split into two main sections, these are 'Computing Mathematics' & 'Computer Systems'. The topic of Computing Mathematics is pretty self explanatory as its the mathematical concepts that underpins computer. This subject is split various maths topics:

  • Sets
  • Matrices & Vectors
  • Functions
  • Logic & Predicate Logic
  • Graph Theory
The Computer Systems topic covers the 'low-end' design of computers, this provides a solid base for understanding computers in much more detail.
